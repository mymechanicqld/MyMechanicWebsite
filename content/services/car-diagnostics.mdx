## When you need diagnostics

Modern cars run dozens of sensors and a handful of computers. When something goes wrong, the car flags it on the dashboard as a warning light, often without telling you what is actually broken. Some common scenarios we diagnose every week:

- The check-engine light came on and will not go away
- The car is running rough or losing power on hills
- Fuel economy has dropped noticeably in the last month
- The transmission has started shifting late or harshly
- The ABS or traction-control light is on
- The car keeps stalling or has trouble starting hot
- A new noise has appeared (ticking, knocking, whining)

Some of these have simple fixes. Some hint at expensive repairs. The point of a proper diagnostic is to tell you the difference before anyone replaces a part.

## What's included

A full diagnostic from us covers:

- Reading all fault codes from every available control module (engine, transmission, ABS, airbags, body)
- Pulling freeze-frame data and historical codes for intermittent faults
- Live data testing of the relevant sensors (oxygen sensors, MAF, MAP, coolant temp, fuel trims)
- A physical inspection of related components (vacuum hoses, connectors, obvious leaks and corrosion)
- A clear written explanation of what we found and what it means
- A fixed-price quote for the repair, with the diagnostic fee credited against it if you proceed

We do not just clear codes and hand the car back. Cleared codes always come back if the underlying fault is still there.
