## When you need a logbook service

There are two reasons to keep up with logbook servicing:

**Warranty protection.** Under the Australian Consumer Law, your new-car warranty stays intact as long as the car is serviced to manufacturer specification by a qualified mechanic, using appropriate parts. Skipping services or using the wrong parts is the only way you genuinely lose warranty cover. The dealership cannot refuse warranty just because someone else did the service.

**Resale value.** A fully-stamped logbook adds real money to your trade-in or sale price. Most private buyers will not look at a car without one. Most dealers will discount their offer if the book has gaps.

The logbook itself tells you the schedule. Service every 10,000 to 15,000 km or six to twelve months, whichever comes first. We follow your specific manufacturer's intervals exactly.

## What's included

**Minor service (most common, every 10,000 to 15,000 km):**

- Engine oil change to manufacturer specification
- New oil filter (OEM or quality aftermarket)
- Tyre pressure check and adjustment
- Top up windscreen washer fluid and coolant if needed
- Multi-point safety inspection (brakes, suspension, lights, belts, hoses, leaks)
- Logbook stamp and itemised invoice emailed to you
- Reset of the service reminder on the dash

**Major service (every 30,000 to 60,000 km depending on make):**

Everything in a minor service, plus:

- New air filter
- New cabin (pollen) filter
- Brake fluid check, flush if due
- Spark plugs on petrol engines if due
- Differential and transfer-case oil check on 4WDs
- Deeper inspection of CV boots, exhaust, fuel lines
- Battery health test

If we find anything outside the scheduled service items, we stop, take a photo, and call you with a fixed quote. Nothing extra goes on the bill without your agreement.
