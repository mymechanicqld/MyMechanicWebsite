## When you need this service

A car that will not start is the most stressful thing your car can do. School run, work meeting, hospital visit, it does not matter, the timing is always terrible. The good news is that the cause is almost always one of three things, and we can tell you which one in about half an hour.

The three culprits, ranked by how often they are at fault:

1. **The battery.** Usually because it is old, has been sitting flat after lights left on, or has died from a hot Queensland summer. Batteries typically last three to five years here.
2. **The alternator.** It charges the battery while the engine runs. When it fails, the battery slowly drains during driving, and the car eventually will not start. You often see a battery light on the dash days or weeks before it dies completely.
3. **The starter motor.** It cranks the engine when you turn the key. When it fails, you hear a single click or a slow grinding crank, but the dashboard lights stay bright (which rules out the battery).

A workshop will often replace whichever part you arrived for, take your money, and discover later that it was actually one of the other two. We test all three on-site before we recommend anything.

## What's included

The standard charging-system diagnostic includes:

- Battery state-of-charge test (resting voltage and load test)
- Battery state-of-health test
- Alternator output test under load (idle, mid-revs and electrical-load applied)
- Starter-motor current draw test
- Drive-belt and tensioner inspection
- Parasitic-draw test if requested (for cars that keep going flat overnight)

If we find the fault, we quote the replacement on the spot. If it is a clean-cut alternator or starter, we usually have the right part on the van for common makes. For less common vehicles we order the correct part and book a return visit, often the same week.
